Tips

Focus on compound exercises

Compound exercises like squats, deadlifts, and bench presses work multiple muscle groups at once, leading to more muscle growth and hypertrophy.

Lift heavy weights

To promote muscle hypertrophy, you need to lift weights that are heavy enough to challenge your muscles. Aim for weights that allow you to complete 7-12 reps with good form.

Use progressive overload:

Gradually increase the weight you lift over time to continually challenge your muscles and promote hypertrophy. Notting down your personal record reps weight can help with this tip

Get enough rest

Adequate rest and recovery is essential for muscle growth. Make sure to take rest days and get enough sleep to support your body's muscle-building processes.

Stay consistent:

Consistency is key when it comes to promoting muscle hypertrophy. Stick to a regular workout schedule and follow your nutrition plan consistently to see results over time.

Follow a balanced nutrition plan:

Eating a balanced diet that includes enough protein, carbohydrates, and healthy fats is crucial for supporting muscle hypertrophy. Consider consulting with a registered dietitian to create a nutrition plan that supports your goals.
