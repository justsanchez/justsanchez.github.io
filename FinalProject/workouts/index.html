To work out the upper body, focus on compound exercises like push-ups, pull-ups, and rows. Don't forget isolation exercises for specific muscles. Maintain proper form, gradually increase weight and intensity, and allow for rest and recovery time.

Chest/Tricep Based Workouts

Dumbbell Press

Muscles worked: Shoulders, Chest, Triceps

expand screen to show more information...

Description:

  • Lie on a flat bench with your feet planted on the ground and your head, back, and butt touching the bench.
  • Hold a pair of dumbbells with your palms facing forward and your arms extended over your chest, with your elbows slightly bent.
  • Lower the dumbbells down to the sides of your chest, while keeping your elbows tucked in close to your body.
  • Exhale as you press the weights back up to the starting position, fully extending your arms but not locking out your elbows.
  • Inhale as you slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the sides of your chest.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Shoulder Press

Muscles worked: Shoulders, Triceps

expand screen to show more information...

Description:

  • Lift the weights to shoulder height, with your elbows bent and pointing outward.
  • Tighten your core, keep your back straight, and exhale as you press the weights up above your head.
  • Fully extend your arms, but do not lock out your elbows.
  • Inhale as you slowly lower the weights back to shoulder height, maintaining control.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Dumbbell Flys

Muscles worked: Chest (Pectoralis Major)

expand screen to show more information...

Description:

  • Lie flat on a bench with your feet planted firmly on the floor and a dumbbell in each hand.
  • Hold the dumbbells above your chest with your arms extended and palms facing each other.
  • Slowly lower the dumbbells out to the sides, keeping a slight bend in your elbows and keeping them in line with your shoulders, until you feel a stretch in your chest.
  • Pause briefly, then exhale and bring the dumbbells back up to the starting position, squeezing your chest muscles at the top of the movement.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Cable Tricep Pulldowns

Muscles worked: Triceps

expand screen to show more information...

Description:

  • Attach a straight bar to a high pulley on a cable machine and adjust the weight according to your fitness level.
  • Stand facing the cable machine with your feet shoulder-width apart, and grasp the bar with an overhand grip, hands shoulder-width apart.
  • Keeping your elbows close to your body, exhale and pull the bar down towards your thighs, extending your arms fully and squeezing your triceps at the bottom of the movement.
  • Inhale and slowly return the bar back to the starting position with a controlled movement, keeping a slight bend in your elbows.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Bench Press

Muscles worked: Chest (Pectoralis Major), Triceps, Shoulders (Anterior Deltoids)

expand screen to show more information...

Description:

  • Lie flat on a bench with your feet planted firmly on the floor and a loaded barbell racked above you.
  • Grasp the barbell with a grip that is slightly wider than shoulder-width apart, and unrack it from the rack.
  • Lower the barbell down to your chest with a controlled movement, keeping your elbows at a 45-degree angle to your body and your shoulder blades retracted.
  • Pause briefly at the bottom of the movement, then exhale and press the barbell back up to the starting position, squeezing your chest muscles and triceps at the top of the movement.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Arms Based Workouts

Dumbbell Curls

Muscles worked: Biceps, Forearms

expand screen to show more information...

Description:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand with your palms facing forward and your arms fully extended at your sides.
  • Keeping your elbows close to your body, exhale and curl the dumbbells up towards your shoulders, contracting your biceps at the top of the movement.
  • Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then inhale and slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position with a controlled movement.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Reverse Dumbbell Curls

Muscles worked: Forearms, Brachioradialis

expand screen to show more information...

Description:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a pair of dumbbells with an overhand grip, palms facing down.
  • Let your arms hang by your sides with your elbows slightly bent.
  • Keeping your elbows tucked in close to your body, exhale and slowly curl the weights up towards your shoulders, while keeping your palms facing down.
  • Inhale and slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Hammer Curls

Muscles worked: Biceps, forearms, and grip strength.

expand screen to show more information...

Description:

  • Begin by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart and holding a pair of dumbbells at your sides with your palms facing your body and your elbows close to your torso.
  • Keeping your elbows close to your sides, slowly curl the weights up towards your shoulders, keeping your palms facing your body throughout the movement.
  • Pause for a brief moment at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Seated Incline Dumbbell Curls

Muscles worked: Biceps, forearms, and grip strength.

expand screen to show more information...

Description:

  • Set an incline bench to a comfortable angle and sit on the bench with your back against the backrest.
  • Hold a pair of dumbbells at your sides with your palms facing forward and your elbows close to your torso.
  • Keeping your elbows close to your sides, slowly curl the weights up towards your shoulders, keeping your palms facing forward throughout the movement.
  • Pause for a brief moment at the top of the movement, then slowly lower the weights back down to the starting position.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Back Based Workouts

Bent Over Dumbbell Rows

Muscles worked: Upper and mid-back muscles, biceps, forearms

expand screen to show more information...

Description:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand with your palms facing each other and your arms fully extended at your sides.
  • Hinge forward at your hips and bend your knees slightly, keeping your back straight and your chest up. Your torso should be almost parallel to the floor.
  • Exhale and pull the dumbbells up towards your sides, squeezing your shoulder blades together and keeping your elbows close to your body.
  • Pause briefly at the top of the movement, then inhale and slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position with a controlled movement.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Reverse Dumbbell Flys

Muscles worked: Rear deltoids, upper back muscles, rotator cuff muscles

expand screen to show more information...

Description:

  • Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell in each hand with your palms facing each other and your arms fully extended at your sides.
  • Hinge forward at your hips and bend your knees slightly, keeping your back straight and your chest up. Your torso should be almost parallel to the floor.
  • Exhale and lift the dumbbells out to the sides, keeping your elbows slightly bent and your palms facing down.
  • Squeeze your shoulder blades together at the top of the movement, then inhale and slowly lower the dumbbells back down to the starting position with a controlled movement.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Face Pulls

Muscles worked: Rear deltoids, upper back muscles, rotator cuff muscles, trapezius muscles, rhomboids

expand screen to show more information...

Description:

  • Set up a cable machine with a rope attachment at chest height. Stand facing the machine and grasp the rope with both hands, with your palms facing each other and your arms extended straight out in front of you.
  • Take a step back to create tension on the cable, and position your feet shoulder-width apart with a slight bend in your knees.
  • Exhale and pull the rope towards your face, keeping your elbows high and squeezing your shoulder blades together at the end of the movement.
  • Hold the contraction for a brief moment, then inhale and slowly return the rope back to the starting position with a controlled movement.
  • Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.
